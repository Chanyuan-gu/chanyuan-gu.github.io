About me!

Hello! I’m currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate working with Fumiko Hoeft in the Department of Psychological Sciences at the University of Connecticut. I completed my PhD with Ping Li at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University.

My research interests center around the neurocognitive mechanisms of language comprehension in bilingual speakers and children. Using multimodal data and various neuroimaging techniques, I aim to uncover how the brain supports reading, listening, and audiovisual comprehension, along with the impacts of individual differences. I am also interested in the association between human brains and large language models during language comprehension. Ultimately, my goal is to gain a deeper understanding of how people learn and use language.
